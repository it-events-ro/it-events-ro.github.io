Lean Development for Startups Workshop

miercuri, 3 iulie 2013

Timisoara Startup Hub is partnering in organizing a workshop on Lean Development for Startups. Please note that besides RSVPing to this event you are supposed to register on Eventbrite and to pay a 7 EUR participation fee intended to cover the costs incurred by organizing the event.

Explore how to develop a great idea into a great company. Learn how to identify opportunities based on real customer needs. Develop a solid business model. Create a successful venture.

JOIN the workshop on Lean Development for start-ups which will be held on Tuesday 2nd of July in Timisoara.


OVERVIEW
• Number of Participants: 60 people
• Event Duration: 4 hours
• Price of the Event: 30 Lei


INTRO
For 50 years we have been told the wrong approach on how to start a business. Long business plans, endless market researches and imaginary financial forecasts have done nothing but waste the resources of young entrepreneurs, who have often neglected the study of the most important aspect: the understanding of Who the customer is and the co-creation of a solution that effectively responds to his needs and challenges.


WHO CAN PARTICIPATE
The event is open to anyone, from students interested in learning about the Lean Start up approach to early stage start-ups, as well as established companies willing to challenge their current model.
It does not matter in what stage participants are with their project, because regardless of the stage, there are always assumptions to be tested.


WORKSHOP STRUCTURE
The workshop is divided into 4 parts:

1. Concepts of Lean Start-Up (30min): Lean vs traditional strategic planning; value vs waste, Validated Learning, We will go through the definitions of success that matter to startups, how to test key business assumptions, pivoting, uncovering the customers archetype, Value Proposition Canvas, Minimum Viable Product, key performance indicators, the funnel, Split testing, Lean Canvas (Ash Murya), the Business Model Canvas (Alexander Osterwalder).
The paradigm shift (10min): the importance of communities, co-creating value with customers and supporters, crowd-sourcing, the power of openness.

2. Business Modeling (50min): participants are given a 20 questions methodology to structure their business model. The necessary theoretical knowledge is introduced to enable better understanding of each question. Participants will be given 1 minute per question: the goal is to focus on key words. Participants without a project will join an existing project and work together to answer all questions.

3. Hypotheses Validation (100 min): participants form groups of 4 people and each individual project is given 10 to 15 minutes to present his business model to the rest of the group. Entrepreneurs will benefit from the other start-ups open feedback to draw the most effective strategy to validate key business assumptions and build their Minimum Viable Product (MVP). Mentors will facilitate the collaborative process guiding the debate towards the creation of solutions which can address the start-ups challenges.

4. Elevator’s Pitch (40 min): participants will have 120 seconds to pitch their idea in front of the workshop’s audience.


THE TRAINERS
The course will be held by Emanuele Musa and Ruxandra Creosteanu.

Emanuele Musa is graduated in electronic engineering at the Politecnico di Torino, and in International Management at the European School of Management ESCP (London - Paris - Rio de Janeiro); he currently holds workshops on Business Modeling throughout Europe and is launching two start-ups – Babele and Democracity – in Bucharest, Romania.

Previous work experience include: Project Planning and Management for Procter & Gamble in Paris, financial analysis at the Fund Manager "AE Global Investment Solutions ltd" in London, strategic analysis at Bombardier Transportation in Brussels, strategy consulting at Advention Business Partners in Paris and management consulting at IBM in Paris.

Ruxandra Creosteanu is graduated in International Management at the European School of Management ESCP (Paris - London - Berlin), and she holds together with Emanuele the Start-up workshops. In parallel she works as a director for Carrefour Romania and is responsible for the business development of the above mentioned Start-ups. Ruxandra speaks fluently 5 languages and she previously worked as a manager consultant within the sustainability team at Deloitte France.


ABOUT BABELE
The sponsor is the non-profit association Babele, which aims to use collective intelligence to support innovation and social innovation, enabling the transfer of best practices and the deployment of viable businesses through open collaboration.
The association main activity is the development of an online platform where young entrepreneurs follow a simple and intuitive methodology to create their own business model, receiving continues support from the online community of customers, mentors and supporters.
Through this system, entrepreneurs from all over the planet structure, share and develop their ideas, putting a solid foundation for their long term viability. They learn how to identify business opportunities based on the actual needs of customers and to develop a business model that can create value and grow sustainably over time.
