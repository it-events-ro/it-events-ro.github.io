Timisoara Mobile Development Group February Meetup

luni, 10 februarie 2014

As for the January edition, we'll have this month a mixed edition with contributions from both locals and special guests from abroad.

The agenda will include:

- a presentation by Paul Ardeleanu ((a London-based mobile and web developer and tutor) on BETFUZE , a social betting project recently launched in UK. As you probably imagine BETFUZE addresses the data-dense betting industry, relying on a multi-server Ruby architecture to provide a back-end processing solution and providing highly personalizable interface, designed to learn from user's behavior.


- a presentation by Adrian Pasarica and Mircea Rila , the CEO and the CTO of Parudi, the startup that developed Spot the Number , on how the 3 months period they've spent in Sofia as beneficiaries of the investment and acceleration services offered by Eleven influenced the evolution of their app on both technical and business side. Adi and Mircea will offer insights on what kind of mobile apps and teams are sought for by accelerators, which were the most important things the app and team gained through participation to the acceleration program, how a multi-platform gaming app evolves in the various platforms, how it keeps the unique look and feeling across the platforms, what user specificity can be identified, how update publishing processes are managed by the stores, how the game performs from the financial side on various platform. We promise this will be just a teaser for a Timisoara Startup Hub event fully dedicated to Parudi/Spot the Number participation to Eleven acceleration program.

- a presentation by Daniel Franc ( Google Developer Groups ) and Alex Blogu ( Google Romania ) , followed by discussions on how Google is planning to re-build its GDGs (Google Developer Group). Having Android in an important position in the diverse landscape of Google technologies, mobile developers' opinions on re-shaping the Timisoara Google Developer Group and subsequent involvement are pretty significant.


- the evening will be concluded by a presentation by Marian-Emanuel Ionascu si Iasmina Gruicin, on Hi-Breathe , a solution for air quality monitoring. The app, developed in order to candidate to represent Timisoara and Romania to Imagine Cup 2014, aims to raise awareness about the main health aspects that can be affected by air pollution. Hi-Breathe is a hardware and software solution, built around the Internet of Things concept. In this direction, the project is composed of several smart devices, easy to use in our daily life routine. Data collected from these devices is brought to the user through an intuitive and user-friendly Windows Phone 8 application.
