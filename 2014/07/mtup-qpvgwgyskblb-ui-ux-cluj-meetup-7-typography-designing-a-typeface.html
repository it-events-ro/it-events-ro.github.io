UI/UX Cluj Meetup#7 - Typography & Designing a Typeface

marți, 8 iulie 2014

Hi there!

We're kicking off this month with a new UI/UX meetup and with a subject close to all our hearts and eyes especially. It's a theme we've been long waiting for and it's fascinating because it finds itself so much in the concept and creation area.

Next week we're going to discuss

95% of the information on the web is written language. It is only logical to say that a web designer should get good training in the main discipline of shaping written information, in other words: Typography.

Daniel Muntean and Iulian Corunga are going to be in the spotlight this time.

Daniel Muntean has been a Graphic Designer for more than six years, mostly doing freelance work and trying new job positions from time to time. Since 2013, he’s an UX Designer at Yonder, challenging himself into this curious and exciting field. When designing interfaces gets tedious, he’s in his studio producing Electronic Music or taking photographs of playful shadows.

After years of working with Typography, Daniel has decided to create one himself, and took the opportunity of the BA Diploma at UAD to do so. He is going to share that experience with us, and provide an in-depth view of the process of Designing a Typeface from scratch.

Also on Tuesday, Inya Şerban and Vlad Pârvu will bring us some examples of typefaces made ​​by them.

Inya Şerban has a B.A. and a M.A. in Communication and PR. For her, art is a passion that she has since she was little and which manifested through drawing, creative writing an dramatic art. This leads her to organize two exhibitions of Japanese art and to became a trainer and a brand consultant. Inya believes that, eventually, communication is also an art.

Vlad Pârvu has a B.F.A. in Design from the University of Art and Design of Cluj-Napoca and he's passionate about photography, painting, drawing and music. He worked as a consultant and designer for various projects in the United Arab Emirates and now he lives in Cluj working in the fashion design industry.

Together they opened a small business that they want to grow big, because they discovered they have a common passion: fashion design. They work hard to bring something new on the market, and all their free time is also dedicated to art.

For all Typography enthusiasts out there, it’s going to be fun! :)
