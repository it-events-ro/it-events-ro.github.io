About The Next Web

The Next Web is one of Europe's most important web technology conferences in Eastern Europe, taking place once a year in Amsterdam. The 7th edition of the conference will be taking place in April 2012, expecting an estimate of 1800 people. The conference's audience is a blend of decision makers from the European & American tech scene, developers, start-ups, innovators, along with venture capitalists, industry journalist, bloggers, and senior level executives.

The Next Web goes back to 2006 when Boris, Arjen and Patrick organized the first conference. It was planned as a one-off to promote their little startup named Fleck.com. But with 300+ attendees and a great line-up of speakers – Kevin Kelly, Kevin Rose and Michael Arrington among others – it turned out a lot more successful than they imagined. After this first conference Patrick and his partners realized they had something that people were waiting for. So they promptly started organizing The Next Web Conference 2007 and it had grown ever since. Last conference in 2011 had over 1200 participants and 32  speakers from 40 countries.

About Patrick de Laive

Patrick is an internet entrepreneur and evangelist and a true web enthusiast. He is the initiator of one of Europe’s largest web conferences - The Next Web - and the correspondent online publication that specialises in latest news about Internet technology, business and culture and he also started OpenCoffee Amsterdam and co-organized Kings of Code. Patrick also co-founded TwitterCounter, feest.je and Paydro. He’s investor in social software platform Wakoopa.com and music startup Twones.
