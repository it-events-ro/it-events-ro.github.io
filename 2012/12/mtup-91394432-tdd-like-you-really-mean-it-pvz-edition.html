TDD like you really mean it - PVZ edition

marți, 4 decembrie 2012

This past summer, we did two Agile coding sessions during which we test-drove not only the development but also the solution design for a simple problem domain: Tic-Tac-Toe.
Feedback from the participants was overwhelmingly positive and we are now stepping up the game.
This time our problem domain is going to be the popular game of Plants vs. Zombies.

Our objectives for this event:
Learn the purest form of Test Driven Development.
Find out how to let your solution design be guided entirely by tests.
Build an application starting from atomic behaviors instead of a BDUF (big design upfront).

There will be a short presentation at the beginning, then we write code in sessions of 45 minutes each. During the breaks we discuss our designs, brainstorm improvements and learn about each other's innovative approaches.

Please bring with you a laptop with a working development and testing environment for your programming language of choice. This event will be language agnostic. You should also have git or equivalent version control software.
One last prerequisite: you should be familiar with the basic gameplay for Plants vs. Zombies. This video might be useful to those who have never played the game:
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lPie0xqd2Rk
The game is available free of charge in many forms, one of which is a Chrome extension which redirects to:
http://chrome.plantsvszombies.com/
