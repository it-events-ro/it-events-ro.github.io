Wearable computing - design for a new frontier?

sâmbătă, 12 octombrie 2013

We all agree that design massively helps businesses to create value for their clients by contributing to creation of better products and services. Futurologists are saying that we have embarked on a promising disruption, the wearable computing. It is already considered a stand alone field, with tremendous growth predictions for the near feature. It will affect our lifestyle, it will have a big cultural impact and it is expected to even significantly change the way society functions. There is no doubt that design will play a central role to this revolution, by answering to various challenges related to the creation of human centered devices, apps and experiences. In order to address such challenges, designers must be holistic thinkers, and they need to act across domains, considering and including aspects from applied social sciences, applied life science, chemistry, materials science, intelligent fabrics, computer science, etc. In order to emulate this processes we are inviting interested students and young professionals from various fields to attend over the two successive Saturdays of October 12th and 19th, 14.00-17.00, in Timisoara Startup Hub, the first wearable computing design workshop in Romania. In a hands-on approach, most used designers’ methods to problem solving: research, ideation, prototyping will be used in an event co-created and facilitated by Adonis Raduca and Radu Ticiu. During the workshop we’ll brainstorm for identifying situations in people’s lives where wearable devices usage could bring significant improvement. With the most challenging situations identified, participants will create multidisciplinary teams that will ideate, define and refine concepts of wearable computing based products and services and will develop corresponding prototypes. We are looking forward to have an interesting experience that will open interesting ways for further developments that could translate in the creation of a local wearable computing community, emergence of some startup project or research initiatives.
