SALES TRAINING WORKSHOP Preparatory Roundtable

vineri, 27 decembrie 2013

US & Canada are among the biggest buyers of products and services globally and offer sustained growth opportunities to startups all across the world. Competing with North American service providers and established businesses, how can a Romanian startups succeed in capturing a piece of this lucrative market? Timisoara Startup Hub is planning a training series for Romanian startups and sales professionals interested in competing and succeeding in the North American market without having to relocate. Our first event is expected to be held in February/March of 2014 at the Timisoara Startup Hub. The trainer is Urooj Qureshi, a young professional, Canadian citizen, with a diverse background around tech industries (details below). INVITATION As a Romanian startup entrepreneur or sales professional dealing with a global potential product or service, we invite you to attend an informal and friendly atmosphere preparatory roundtable discussion on December 27, 2013, 3pm to share your ideas and provide input to help shape the training sessions that will be held in 2014. By participating you can help create a program that will benefit your learning and cover the topics that you see most important to Romanian startups. The participation is free and will offer you special advantages for participating in the 2014 Spring training. Please confirm your participation by December 24, 5.00 pm. THE TRAINER Urooj Qureshi is a serial entrepreneur from Canada with more than a decade's experience in building successful organizations in the North American market. Urooj has been a six time start-up founder and an advisor to many young and established companies. He also been a speaker at prestigious events including the "Art of the Start" and "CCSR Conference" - Canada's largest event on Corporate and Community Social Responsibility. Urooj's relationship driven approach to business has earned him the "Notable Networker" award by Business Network International and a seat on the “Advisory Board” for the School of Business at Algonquin College.
