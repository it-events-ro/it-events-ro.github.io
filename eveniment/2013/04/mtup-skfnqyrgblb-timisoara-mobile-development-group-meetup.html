Timisoara Mobile Development Group Meetup

luni, 15 aprilie 2013

This April we'll talk at our Timisoara Mobile Development Group meetup about three topics.

http://photos3.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/4/2/0/6/event_222796902.jpegThe first, opening presentation will be delivered by Catalin Gheorghiu , Microsoft Most Valuable Professional Device Application Development and Windows Embedded, will approach an unexpected Azure Mobile Services for... lazy engineers topic.

Additionally to this topic, Catalin will also present an April 27 event he will coordinate: a Timisoara implementation of the Global Windows Azure Bootcamp , an one-day deep dive class that will get you up to speed on developing for Windows Azure. The class includes a training and a series of labs to practice what was learned.

As Catalin also recently became a Nokia Developer Champion, he will share with us what advantages can he transfer to the members of our mobile developers community.

</a><a href="http://www.amvtek.com/">

The second presentation will bring in the spotlight the AMVTek team ( Teodora Vreme ,  Marc Vieuille, Nicu Sur du ) that are ready to lunch their Cab Surf, a mobile interface between passengers and cab transportation providers.

The Cab Surf has already it's both driver and passenger Android versions and iOS versions available in the respective app stores so you will most probably be ready to use them after this presentation.

For the third point on the agenda, Silviu Vert and Radu Ticiu will give a teaser presentation of Timisoara ECAhack, a hackathon planned for the weekend of May 24-26, as part of a Eastern Europe and Central Asia series of events based on a concept developed by Dan Romescu.

The ECAhack will be preceded by a series of trainings including a first one focusing on UI/UX for mobile, to be delivered by Adonis Raduca , Saturday April 20th and several other to be announced soon. Also as part of this presentation, Dan Bugariu , one of the leaders of Timisoara Java User Group , will present the ' City Projects ' concept, an initiative which encourages specialists to involve into the development of apps for solving problems of the local community and enhance the existing services.

We'll keep you updated if any exchanges will appear.
