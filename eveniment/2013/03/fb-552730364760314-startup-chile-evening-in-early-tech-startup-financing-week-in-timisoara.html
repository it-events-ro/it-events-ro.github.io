Startup Chile Evening in (Early Tech) Startup Financing Week in Timisoara

luni, 11 martie 2013

Join us for a lively discussion on Startup Chile, one of the best startup support programs in the world! The success in founding a startup and in meaningfully using one’s energies, knowledge and time towards its development is significantly affected by the possibility to finance the early stages of this process. Bootstrapping or keeping the new project on a side lane along the 9 to 5 job could provide some solutions in this respect, but such an approach will definitely increase the “time to market” or the “time to success” (no matter how the success is defined) but also the odds of project to be aborted before a launch. This applies to all geographies but is significantly more difficult in Romania where the available resources are pretty limited. Between the 11th and 15th of March, Timisoara Startup Hub offers you a set of opportunities to learn about ways to attract financial resources in your startup: Monday, March 11, 6.30 PM, we’ll talk with Denis Todirica about Startup Chile, one of the most coherent and globally recognized startup support programs, devised by the government of Chile to share the enthusiasm and stamina of up to 1000 startups in order to enhance the dynamics of the local ecosystem. As a Startup Chile beneficiary, a team can receive a grant of up to 40k USD (so no equity “loss” involved), to finance its functioning in Santiago or other country’s major cities, access local talent, consultancy, events, contacts, mentoring and coaching and eventually showcase to investors their prototype, developed at the end of the up to 6 months program. As one of the few Romanian participants of Startup Chile, Denis will present the general info on the program, Glazeon’s (his own startup) experiences in Chile, info about the other 4-5 Romanian projects admitted and will give recommendations on how to prepare and submit a successful applications under the current call opening March 11th and having a deadline April 9th. The event is open for students, current or to-be entrepreneurs, employees dreaming about changing their career path. Check our Facebook page for info about other events within the (Early Tech) Startup Financing Week in Timisoara.
