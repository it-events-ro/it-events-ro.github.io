TiMo Android Training Series for Young Developers

joi, 1 august 2013

IMPORTANT NOTICE 2: Unfortunately we are forced to postpone the training. It is most probably to start it during the Autumn months. Until then, please express your interest by joining the event. We'll keep all registered informed about any new development.

IMPORTANT NOTICE: Your registration is only valid upon filling in the following form .

What?

TiMo starts a series of trainings targeted to about 10 West Romania young programmers (not older than 26) interested in Android development, delivered by Andrei Marcut , an enthusiast mobile development expert and trainer, with a solid mobile product and business development background. Following the participation to this project organized by Timisoara Mobile Development Group, those interested should build a strong mobile, Android foundation or strengthen an existing one.

More specifically, the training is aiming to teach participants in the following areas of expertize, from brief theory sustained by practical examples, to hands-on exercises & homework.

General skills :

Mobile-oriented principles

Requirements gathering, analysis & negociation

Team work and team tools/utilities

Project budgeting and task planning

Engineering, not overengineering

Technical skills:

Android - development tools

Android - development lifecycle

Android - application fundamentals

Android - core development

Android - APIs, sensors, libraries

Android - memory management, memory leaks, logging and debugging

Android - development practice, implementation

Android - best practices, standards, design patterns, antipatterns

Android - extreme development

Participants will have the chance to learn and develop the above mentioned skills and further on, based on their enthusiasm, technical fundamentals, paid attention and time invested doing homeworks & beyond.

Where?

The trainings will take place in Timisoara Startup Hub or in other spaces within the City Business Centre.

Prerequisites (will be evaluated through an initial admission test):

Must have:
Technical English, strong OOP principles understanding, Java language basics, good algorithmics, interest & enthusiasm.
A computer connected to the internet, to work on at home with an Android emulator installed.

Nice to have:
Version Control (git, svn), XML, SQL, Design Patterns, previous experience with mobile development platforms or tools, OOP practice

No laptops nor mobile phones will be needed at the training.

When?

The first training session will take place Saturday, May 11th, starting at 3PM.

Format

The sessions will take place in 6 to 8 Saturdays, in the 3-6 PM interval, loosely distributed over two months (there will be some break weeks).

Each session will be build around a major topic that will be presented by the lecturer, with relevant practical examples. A set of “problems” will be defined as homework. Participants will have to submit their homework for review prior to the next session, when they will be publicly discussed by the lecturer with the class.

Each session will conclude with a Q&A segment. Participants will be encouraged to collect and to share resources within the homework solving process.

Platforms like StackOverflow or Git will be used for co-operative working and lecturer-student communication.

During the training, the participants' group will jointly develop an application responding to the needs of a real-life organization.
