Argentinian Violin

duminică, 29 noiembrie 2015

Argentinian Violin Ce ? Argentina has been one of the very very few places where people from all over the world found shelter, felt at home, stayed and lived together,all mixed up and in peace. It is said that "Argentina is the richest country ever,for its natural resources,the only negative thing are the Argenines”. That`s of course not true, and in fact that`s what my country is all about,about the Argentines that actually make Argentina possible and so incredibly unique. So, I grew up in this multicultural place,of course without knowing what that meant. It was just like that! Then I started traveling and eventually I moved to Europe. After a couple of years away from Argentina, I carry with me stories, images, sensations, feelings and sounds from almost every place I lived in. It might sound funny, but the more I get to know the varied european cultures, the more reminiscences I find from them in me and in my homeland. The Atelier is all about getting to know each other,and also to discover some new things about the amazing and intriguing Argentina! There are many storis to tell and many sounds to play on the violin! Cu cine? With Francisco, an Argentine that plays the violin and is willing to learn Romanian from Romanians, of course! A nomad Argentine, actually! Thank you for this beautiful opportunity and for having thought of me. It feels really good not to be alone in Bucuresti, specially after only one month since I got back here. Cu ce? I would say you should bring everything you know or may have heard about Argentina, but please do take your preconceptions with you! We will try to give some answers to those questions together! Bring with you questions! I wish that it will be more of a dialog, instead of being only me the one that speaks :) Când? Duminică@incubator107 , 29 Noiembrie de la 19:00 la 21:00 Unde? Atelierul se ţine în mansarda noastră din str. Traian 107 (Zona Hala Traian), sector 2, București. Dacă nu ştii deja, află cum să ajungi: http://bucuresti.incubator107.com/contact.html. Cum?
