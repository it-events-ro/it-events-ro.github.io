Sounds of the Untold. Balkan Melodies.

duminică, 11 octombrie 2015

Ce ? Sevdah is a word retaken from Turkish language, with Arabic rooths, „sawda” meaning „black gall”. Black gall in the Ancient times was believed to be one of the four basic substances in our body, responsable for emotional behaviour, provocing states of melancholy and irritation. Melancholy itself, coming from Greek, encompasses exactly these two words – black gall. Love was believed to have the same forces. On the other hand, there is the slavic-bogomilian relation towards love in sevdah, which was more of a sweet-nostalgic one believed to be. What it represents in the context of Balkan, namely Bosnia and Herzegovina, as its rooth country and having essencial connection to bosnian traditional music, is hardly explainable in Slavic languages, almost untranslatable to foreign ones, yet, many like to say that, palpable to almost all our senses. Balkan, the land of blood and honey, the homeland of extrems, wars, peace and impossible love stories, hard temper and passion, undoubtedly watered the growth of sevdah, which became a part of the its cultural identity. „In essence, our sevdah is both, the passionate and painful longing of love, as well as the melancholic and sweet one, the feeling when you are incapable of enduring the pain caused by love, and the pain transforms into ecstasy of the intoxication of love that compares to the slow process of dying. Pain, because love cannot be fulfilled at that time, sometimes because space and time act as a wall and obstacle to it, sometimes because there are obstacles of individual, social, familial, traditional or simply emotional and pshychological nature. Sevdah expresses itself as torture by others and oneself, and pleasure of whipping deriving from the identification with the yearning and masochistic experience of love despite if its futility.” (Muhsin Rizvic, Literary historian). Black. Black gall. Passionate and painfull longing. Love yearning. Love. Exstasy of love. And a lot more. That doesnt fit words, but fits music – sevdalinka. On love. Trough filosophy and poetry from Balkan. Trough stories embroidered in their tradition. Trough music to the untold. Cu cine? Aida Sosic is born in Bosnia nad Herzegovina, doctor of dental medicine by proffesion, singer by calling. Alongside dentistry, Aida has been working in theatre for 10 years now, being a collaborator of Teatar FEDRA Bugojno BiH, Altteatar Sarajevo BiH, Compania de Teatru Labirint Bucharest Romania and The Republic of Imagination, international theatre network based in Wales, being particularly interested in exploring the phenomenon of labyrinth theatre.
