Special meetup related to FP7 projects

vineri, 26 august 2011

Program of the meetup:
1) 30 min. talk given by Dr. Elena Simperl : "Collaborative knowledge creation is not a game"
Abstract of the talk: Gamification is one of the most hyped concepts of the past years. Defined as the application of game elements to motivate users to undertake specific activities, it finds more and more applications in public and corporate settings, ranging from artificial intelligence and scientific discovery to recycling and traffic control to office productivity. In this talk we will look into how games and game mechanics have been applied to encourage users to engage with the task of semantic content creation, be that in the form of semantically enabled vertical applications, or on the Semantic Web itself. We will introduce a number of games with a purpose and similar end-user tools which leverage such ideas, as well as best practices and development guidelines for incentives-compatible technology design.
Dr. Elena Simperl works as an assistant professor at the Karlsruhe University of Technology in Germany. She has been active as a Semantic Web researcher for almost a decade, authoring over 75 publications, and serving as chair to several relevant scientific conferences. Elena has been involved in over 15 European and national projects in the field of semantic technologies, including Knowledge Web (EU FP6 NoE), TripCom (EU FP6 STREP), Salero (EU FP6 IP), LarKC (EU FP7 IP), Active (EU FP7 IP), SOA4All (EU FP7 IP), Service Web 3.0 (EU FP7 SA), INSEMTIVES (EU FP7 STREP), PlanetData (EU FP7 NoE), RENDER (EU FP7 STREP), among which she coordinated or is coordinating TripCom, Service Web 3.0, INSEMTIVES, RENDER and PlanetData.


2) 20 min talk given by Juan Pane : "Entitypedia: from linked data to linked entities".
Juan Pane received his degree in Informatics Engineering from the National University of Asuncion, Paraguay. His major research interests include semantic matching, identity management, entity matching and collaborative models for
knowledge evolution in dynamic environments, the latter of which is the main focus of his current Ph.D. studies at the University of Trento in Italy.
