Timișoara Google Developer Group (GDG) Inaugural Meetup

sâmbătă, 8 decembrie 2012

The opening day of the Timișoara Google Developer Group (GDG) will feature a handful of interesting talks for attendees interested in Google's developer technology.

The event is free and open to everyone interested in the subjects discussed. The agenda consists of the following presentations:

Opening Talk - Dan Romescu

http://photos1.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/c/f/0/0/event_183352992.jpegInitiator and co-organizer of the first GDG in this area of our country, Dan Romescu will talk about GDGs all over the world and the opportunities they bring to the local community of developers working with Google technology.

Dan Romescu is a Mobile Social Technology Architect and consummate multicultural business leader with a clear vision for an open, innovative, green and transparent society - right now he is working on social networks metabolism. He has a strong interest in Mobile Augmented Reality, Smart Things and Social Networks Physics, working on a new concept to sustainably increase our lifestyle.

What is a GDG and why are we here?


http://photos2.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/8/3/7/4/event_183393652.jpegThis presentation will touch on subjects such as the purpose of a GDG, what type of events can a GDG organize and the range of themes which can be discussed.

Members of GDG Cluj and GDG Bucharest will share with us their experience in starting and maintaining active groups in their cities.

We will also have some special foreign guests joining our discussion via Google Hangout.

Drawing on Google Maps - Razvan Cioarga

Ever wanted to learn how to draw on Google Maps to enhance your application? This talk will introduce you to Google Maps, the layers of Google Maps and the basics of drawing shapes on Google Maps.

Razvan Cioarga is Teaching Assistant in the Science and Computer Engineering Department at the "Politehnica" University of Timisoara.

Android Crash Course - Introduction & Hello-World - Madalin Gageanu

http://photos1.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/d/a/0/e/event_183355822.jpegAndroid is the most accessible of the mobile platforms out there and Madalin intends to take us on a quick tour of an "Hello World" application, touching in short on subjects like the structure of a project, the basics of activities and intents and how two activities can be linked together.

Madalin Gageanu is a software developer with a varied experience and a passion for graphic and user experience design. Working for the last 2 years as an IT consultant at London based ITHR Consulting, he was involved in proof-of-concept and prototype projects. He's now also a member of the Vodafone Incubation Team in Dusseldorf.

Using Google Drive as a CMS - Aurelian George Dumanovschi

http://photos3.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/d/f/f/4/event_183357332.jpegInstead of building a CMS for a webservice, how about using Google Drive? I'm sure those corporate types know how to use spreadsheets, documents and windows explorer :)

Aurelian Dumanovschi started his own company, Work in Progress, at the end of his bachelor studies, together with 2 of his friends. It was one of the few mobile agencies at that time in Cluj-Napoca. At the moment, the company has 8 employees which focus exclusively on mobile technologies: iOS and Android.

Android - Dealing with Screen Fragmentation - Madalin Gageanu

Fragmentation is a hot topic in the Android universe but dealing with it may not be as painful as it might seem at first look. In this presentation, Madalin will talk about what screen fragmentation is and how the SDK offers ways of dealing with it.

Madalin Gageanu is a software developer with a varied experience and a passion for graphic and user experience design. Working for the last 2 years as an IT consultant at London based ITHR Consulting, he was involved in proof-of-concept and prototype projects. He's now also a member of the Vodafone Incubation Team in Dusseldorf.

Principles of UX - Sorin Bechira

It doesn't matter if you develop mobile apps or desktop apps: the user experience design is just as important. A set of guiding principles is this area is always welcomed.

Sorin Bechira is a thirty-two years old awarded graphic/multimedia designer with a bachelor degree in fine arts, a bachelor in multimedia techniques and new media.

Self motivated, always wanting to experiment and looking for challenges on every field of visual communication: digital matte painting, vfx, graphic design, experimental illustration, typography, interactive and new media, combining traditional techniques with digital ones in order to obtain something unique, something unexpected and spectacular.

Android and Arduino: Big or Small Brothers? - Alexandru Iovanovici

Ever wondered what Android and Arduino can do when used together in one application? Alex will give you some examples and much more.

Alexandru Iovanovici is a Teaching and Research Assistant/Ph.D. Student at the "Politehnica" University of Timisoara. He is also teaching C/C++, database design and web development to high school children at Colegiul National C.D. Loga.

Advanced techniques designing great applications - Eugeniu Arbuleac & Andrei Catinean

Eugen and Andrei from Cluj will give us some tips&tricks on how to make our application efficient, responsive and functional. They will talk about low battery draining, efficient internet flow and so on.

http://photos3.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/8/6/4/4/event_183634372.jpegArbuleac Eugeniu started his way in Android development 2 years ago. Being a student at Technical University of Cluj-Napoca he joined the local Google Developers Group. Now he is involved in organizing GDG related events.

Passionate about technology and design, Eugeniu spends lots of time trying to discover the deepest parts of Android platform, the latest trends in mobile development&design and the way binding them all together in some great applications. He is always seeking for a new, better way of doing his applications.

Andrei Catinean is an Android developer and Java technologies enthusiast at ArtSoft Consult and Co-organizer and speaker at GDG Cluj-Napoca.

Machine translation and Google Translate - Adam Mathias

http://photos3.meetupstatic.com/photos/event/e/2/a/6/event_184318022.jpegThis presentation is an overview of the technology behind machine translation and Google Translate for developers of websites and mobile apps.

Adam Mathias Bittlingmayer is a Software Engineer at Google Translate. Adam has been at Google since 2007. His intellectual passion for languages lead him to Google Translate. Previously he developed business software and in the mobile sector. He is a user and advocate of App Engine. He also participates in Google events for developers in the Middle East and Latin America.
