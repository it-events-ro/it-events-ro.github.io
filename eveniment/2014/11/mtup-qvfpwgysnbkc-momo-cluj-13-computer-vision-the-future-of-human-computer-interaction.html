MoMo Cluj #13 - Computer Vision: The Future of Human-Computer Interaction

luni, 3 noiembrie 2014

In a tribute radio cast to Thomas Edison, Albert Einstein spoke - he said that the technology he had created, this technology is something that people still have to get used to. Referring to the light bulb.


Early technology has brought to man that which made his life comfortable, his house warmer and his travels safer. When being an inventor back in the days, this meant for the creator to make something totally new, that people could not yet believe as being real or useful. Because they were accustomed with the thought that existence was rough and there was nothing that could be done to change this.

But nowadays, technological advancements are of such a subtle nature, that they appear to be very familiar and very natural. Technology is very close to functioning as if it were a real person and interact, respond and solve problems as if it had a mind of its own. If back in the times it was man who needed to get used to new technology, now it is technology that needs to get used to man. And it is only a natural step in the HMI (human-machine interaction) evolution .

AI was very popular starting with the 1990s. Today machine learning, speach recognition and computer vision are fashionable technologies, because they represent the answer to the question: what can we do such that machines respond in as much a natural way as possible?

Scientists, programers and companies throughout the world are fascinated by the endless possibilities that this technology brings and they are focused on exploring it.

Such a company is Tulemod.com - Re-imagining fashion through technology . In today's digital world, Tulemod's mission is to create a platform that uses cutting edge technology, from 3D scanning to database analytics, to re-imagining how users, designers and retailers connect with fashion.

Fashion is a trillion dollar market worldwide. It is an ever growing platform for people to express their creativity and individual styles, yet the fashion industry is archaic, and untouched by modern technologies on all its levels.

Monday, we'll have on-board Radu Orghidan and Mihai Pop from Tulemod Inc. - a company based in Palo Alto, California, that creates a new state of the art digital fashion service: Atelier . The application helps retailers and brands shift from physical to digital inventory and offers the user a unique and engaging online shopping experience.

Radu Orghidan received the Ph.D. degree in Information Technologies from the University of Girona, Spain. He was a postdoctoral researcher in the computer vision field at the TUCN. His research is focused on 3D perception using stereoscopic techniques, 3D interactive tools, image processing, multimedia technologies and eLearning. Currently, he is the CTO of Tulemod.

He will talk about:

- Introduction in 3D reconstruction techniques, reconstruction from multiple images

- Stereo reconstruction methods

- Perspective geometry

- Applications of reconstruction techniques in the fashion world

- Reconstructing and rendering clothes

Ioan Mihai Pop has graduated from the Technical University in Cluj-Napoca (UTCN), getting his license on Computer science in 2012, and currently working on his Masters degree in the same field. He begun his career as a web developer with Microsoft technologies, his very first projects being in ASP MVC, and afterwards taking on projects on mobile Android applications. After growing his experience, he's moved on to projects on 3D technology. As an employee of Tulemod Inc. he is working on developing the Atelier app, meant to deliver a digital fashion service. As he is passionate about electronics and robotics, his free time projects are in the field of 3D printing and RC flying machines.

His presentation will cover:

- Introduction in the technologies used: Unity 3D, 28.io, Amazon s3

- Atelier App Architecture

See you on Monday!
